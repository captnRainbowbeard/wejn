What Are The Advantages And Disadvantages Of Dry And Wet Water Meters?

Jul 21, 2020

On the topic of the advantages and disadvantages of dry water meters and wet water meters, I personally think that we must first understand the difference between dry water meters and wet water meters.


Wet water meter: It means that the counter of the water meter is immersed in water, and the surface glass of the water meter is under water pressure.


Dry-type water meter: a water meter whose counter is not immersed in water. The sensor is technically isolated from the chamber of the counter, and the glass of the water meter is not subject to water pressure.


As for the comparison of the advantages and disadvantages of these two water meters, I feel a bit subjective and one-sided.


1.First of all, regarding the accuracy of measurement, there is no difference between a dry water meter and a wet water meter, and even if there is a difference, it is minimal. This is because any water meter must be tested by an authoritative quality inspection department before use, so users can use it with confidence in terms of measurement accuracy and initial momentum.


2.There is also an article about dry-type water meters that are prone to demagnetization during use. This is an individual phenomenon that occurred in the early production of dry-type water meters, and most of the water meter companies have already solved this problem.


3.Regarding the shortcomings of wet and dry water meters, I think this is a structural defect: after a period of use of wet water meters, the dial is easy to turn yellow and affect the reading; if the surface glass of the water meter freezes and cracks, the water meter will not work or even damage the water meter.


In summary, regarding the pros and cons of these two water meters, I don't think there is a need for a showdown. Because in different use environments, we should correctly choose different water meters. For example: in areas where the water quality is soft and not easy to scale, a wet water meter is suitable. In areas where the water quality is hard and easy to scale, dry water meters are suitable for use, because dry water meters are isolated from the measured water due to their counting mechanism, so there will be no phenomenon that affects reading. Moreover, the stability and safety of dry water meters in cold weather conditions are also significantly higher than that of wet water meters.
