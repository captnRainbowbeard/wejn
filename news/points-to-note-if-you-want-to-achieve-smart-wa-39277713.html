Points To Note If You Want To Achieve Smart Water Use

Oct 30, 2020

We only know that smart water meters have brought us more and more convenient lives. But do you really understand smart water meters? Do you know what to pay attention to when using smart water meters? Below are the things I need to pay attention to when using smart water meters. Come and see which ones you know.

1.What is "prepaid water"?


Prepaid water means that consumers purchase water vouchers in the form of prepaid tokens. When entering the user interface unit (located in the user's home), the token instructs the water management device to allow a certain amount of water to pass through the water meter before shutting down.


Consumers can track usage, remote credit limit, and reduce the possibility of electric shock due to leaks or improper monitoring.


Prepaid water meters can also be used to restrict water flow to specific areas. This helps municipalities and property owners control the amount of water used in certain outputs, and prevents waste by low-income households who cannot afford the excess use of basic needs.


They can pay in small and frequent increments. This prevents them from falling into debt, which may aggravate post-payment arrangements.



2. How does it affect leased properties and legal entities?


The impact on leased properties and legal entities is great.


Prepaid metering reduces the administrator to a minimum, while eliminating the risk and annoyance of delayed or unpaid water bills. This is why the house uses prepaid water as quickly as prepaid electricity.


Gone are the days of dividing the water bill for the entire real estate by the number of units. Prepaid metering means that users only need to pay for their own consumption.
